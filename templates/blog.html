Learn about hacking @ PyOhio'14

A few weeks ago, I went to my first tech conference in Columbus, OH. PyOhio had an infectious spirit. Hundreds of developers with vastly varying degrees of experience were all around: chatting, programming, and giving lectures. Everyone was friendly, sociable, and best of all, humble. I cannot speak enough to how great the attitude was at this conference and I recommend anyone with an interest in tech, or really science for that matter, to attend PyOhio next year.

When I came back home to Cleveland, I put together a presentation for my company to relay some of what I learned with at conference. I talked briefly about Postgres and its centralized notification stream and spent the majority of the time giving a synoposis of my favorite talk of the conference, "The Clean Architecture". I do not believe I did these talks justice (this presentation was given in half the time with much less prep), but I still believe it can relates the largest talking points and takeaways from each talk.
