About Me

I love software. Back in high school, I downloaded and ran a piece of memory scanning software titled "Cheat Engine" in order to hack on web-based flash games; as I watched the stream of hexadecimal addresses flying through the GUI frame, I instantly knew I need to understand what computer wizardry was permitting me to have 9999 attack points.

This interest and passion grew and has now reached far beyond the question of what is happening behind the scenes of a flash game.

I want to know how we, the human race, think and perceive reality. Artificial Intelligence, Computer Vision, Game Theory, Natural Language Processing, etc. These fields are more than tech, they're a study and an implementation of humanity's greatest reaches into the realm of logic.

There isn't a math I enjoy more, and it's why I'll program for work and hobby.

I hope to always work with smart people who enjoy the task of making the menial into cerebral.
